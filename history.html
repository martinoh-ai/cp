Our History

Five centuries of Catalan heritage, lovingly preserved for the modern traveler

A Legacy of Five Centuries

Masia Can Pares stands as a testament to Catalan architectural tradition, its stone walls having witnessed over 500 years of history. Originally built in the 16th century, this traditional farmhouse served generations of local families who worked the surrounding vineyards and olive groves.

The name "Can Pares" derives from the Catalan tradition of naming properties after their founding families, a practice that connects us to our deep roots in this land.

Traditional Catalan Architecture

The masia represents the quintessential Catalan rural dwelling, characterized by its robust stone construction, terracotta roof tiles, and harmonious integration with the landscape. Our property retains many original architectural elements, including hand-hewn wooden beams and traditional arched doorways.

Each restoration effort has been undertaken with deep respect for these historical features, blending modern comfort with centuries-old craftsmanship.

The Botanical Garden

Our 20,000m² botanical garden represents decades of careful cultivation. What was once agricultural land has been transformed into a sanctuary featuring over 600 trees and countless Mediterranean plant species.

The garden serves as a living museum of Mediterranean flora, providing a serene backdrop for contemplation, creativity, and connection with nature.

Through the Centuries

1500s

Foundation

The original masia is constructed, serving as a working farmhouse for local agricultural families.

1800s

Wine Era

The property becomes part of the Penedès wine region's expansion, with vineyards planted throughout the estate.

1900s

Transformation

Agricultural use gradually gives way to residential purposes as the region evolves.

2000s

Restoration

Comprehensive restoration begins, respecting historical integrity while introducing modern amenities.
